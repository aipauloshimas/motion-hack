# Style Library

A menu of distinct, proven collage / motion-graphics looks to assign per beat when the
creator has no reference images (or wants ideas). Each is described by technique, palette
and composition so the model can build it from text alone.

## How to use
- Pick a DIFFERENT style for each beat, for variety across the reel.
- Always render it FULL-FRAME, edge to edge (never a centered patch with empty borders).
- Keep the face recognizable and the mouth coherent and readable (lip-sync safe), even in fragmented styles.
- Describe the look, never name a specific artist.

## No-newspaper-drift guard (important)
Image and video models default to a newsprint collage when you just say "collage." For ANY
style below that is NOT "Newsprint Portrait", add to the prompt: "no newspaper, no newsprint,
no columns of printed text, no magazine-scrap face" so it stays on the chosen style.

## Styles
1. **Geometric Color-Block** — the face rebuilt from bold flat geometric color patches, mismatched bright blocks with clean edges, mid-century silkscreen poster feel.
2. **Street-Poster Mosaic** — a coherent photo face centered, surrounded by a mosaic of torn street posters, foreign-language type, tickets and stamps, on a strong single-color ground (e.g. deep blue).
3. **Glossy Magazine Cut-Out** — face layered from glossy fashion-magazine scraps and headline fragments, editorial, colorful, mixed typography.
4. **Newsprint Portrait** — face built entirely from dense black-and-white newsprint and headline text, halftone print texture, a couple of red or blue accents. (This is the one newspaper look; only pick it on purpose.)
5. **Shattered Photo** — the photographic face sliced into scattered torn rectangular fragments with small gaps and offsets; keep eyes and mouth aligned and readable.
6. **Comic Pop Halftone** — bold black outlines, halftone dot shading, primary colors, comic bursts and "POW" energy, Lichtenstein feel.
7. **Foil Ransom (chrome hip-hop)** — metallic silver and chrome foil textures, ransom-note cut letters, crown or graphic accents, glossy and loud.
8. **Star-Burst Graphic** — playful bold cut-outs, bursting stars and shapes, chunky letters, bright primary colors, high energy.
9. **Op-Art Split** — half real photo, half line-drawing or optical pattern, very high contrast with one accent color (e.g. orange and black), trippy.
10. **Constructivist** — 1920s avant-garde: black, white and one bold accent (yellow), geometric shapes, diagonal bars, torn printed book pages, propaganda-poster look.
11. **Ink Comic Zine** — bold hand-drawn black ink over the photo, energetic linework and scribbles, high-contrast black and white, punk zine.
12. **Minimal Editorial** — mostly clean: a photographic face with just a few torn paper scraps, a stray number or word, lots of negative space, gallery / magazine editorial.
13. **Grunge Typographic** — distressed, deconstructed overlapping torn type, gritty textures, muted ground with one accent, layered and raw.
14. **Cubist Cut-Paper** — vibrant fragmented cubist face from dozens of bright multicolor paper shards, bold outlines, maximal colorful chaos.
15. **Graffiti Scribble** — the photo face overlaid with marker and spray-paint scribbles, tags and drips, a hot accent (pink or lime), raw street energy.

## Pairing styles with the words (optional, nice touch)
When a style naturally echoes the line, use it there: Constructivist or Blueprint for "step by step",
Comic Pop (speech bubble) for "comment", a film/cinema look for "footage", Op-Art or Cubist for a loud "holy shit" hook.
